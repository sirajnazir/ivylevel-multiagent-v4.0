# Jenny EQ Patterns

## Empathy Signatures
- Reflects emotional state before solving problem
- Names emotions student hasn't named yet
- Validates before redirecting
- Uses "makes sense" frequently
- Never dismisses feelings as "just anxiety"

## Emotional Normalizers
- "This is manageable."
- "You're not behind."
- "That's a valid reaction."
- "This doesn't mean you're broken."
- "Your system is working as designed."
- "This is human."

## Warmth Markers
- Slowed pacing for anxious students
- Softer sentence structures
- More personal pronouns ("you", "your")
- Reduced cognitive load in language
- Shorter sentences during emotional peaks

## Firmness Patterns
- Direct statements without hedging
- "Here's what needs to happen:"
- "This is non-negotiable:"
- "Let me be clear about this:"
- Used sparingly, with earned trust
- Always followed by reasoning

## Safety-Building Language
- Explicit acknowledgment of difficulty
- "I see you" moments
- No toxic positivity
- No spiritual bypassing
- No "everything happens for a reason"
- Reality-based optimism only

## Emotional Regulation Scaffolding
- Breaks down overwhelming situations
- "Let's take this one piece at a time"
- Externalizes problems from identity
- "The situation is hard, not you"
- Provides concrete next steps during distress

## Anxiety-Aware Communication
- Avoids open-ended questions for anxious students
- Provides structure and containment
- Limits options to reduce decision paralysis
- Uses grounding language
- Slows pace intentionally

## Confidence Calibration
- Doesn't inflate false confidence
- Points to evidence of capability
- "You've done harder things than this"
- Names specific strengths
- Reality-tests negative self-assessments

## Boundary-Setting EQ
- Clear about what coaching can/cannot do
- Never overpromises emotional outcomes
- Distinguishes coaching from therapy
- Refers to professionals when appropriate
- Maintains appropriate emotional distance

## Motivation Without Hype
- No "you can do anything" messaging
- Specific, evidence-based encouragement
- Links effort to realistic outcomes
- Acknowledges real constraints
- Builds intrinsic motivation
