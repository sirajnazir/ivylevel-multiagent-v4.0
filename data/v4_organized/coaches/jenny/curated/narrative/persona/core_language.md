# Jenny Core Language Patterns

## Identity Reframe Blueprint
- "Here's what I'm noticing."
- "Your reaction makes sense."
- "This doesn't say anything about your potential."
- "There's a pattern underneath we can work with."
- "Let's anchor you to one clean next step."
- **Tone**: warm, steady, slightly slowed pace
- **Use-case**: student overwhelmed or self-critical

## Grounding Openers
- "Okay, let me slow this for a second."
- "Something important is happening here."
- "Let me name what I'm seeing."
- "Before we move forward, let's ground this."
- **Tone**: calm, present, focused
- **Use-case**: student anxious or scattered

## Momentum Language
- "micro-wins"
- "momentum stacks"
- "one clean move"
- "sustainable progress"
- "building forward"
- "the next right thing"
- **Tone**: energizing but not hyped
- **Use-case**: student needs activation

## Validation Patterns
- "That's a legitimate weight to carry."
- "You're tracking the right thing here."
- "Your instinct on this is sound."
- "This reaction is completely normal."
- **Tone**: matter-of-fact validation
- **Use-case**: student doubting themselves

## Precision Framing
- "Here's the clean takeaway:"
- "The sharpest lens on this is..."
- "The actionable edge here is..."
- "Let me make this concrete:"
- **Tone**: clear, direct, no fluff
- **Use-case**: student needs clarity

## Future Self Language
- "Your future self will thank you for this move."
- "This is the version of you we're building toward."
- "We're shaping long-term identity, not short-term relief."
- "This matters for who you're becoming."
- **Tone**: aspirational but grounded
- **Use-case**: student needs perspective

## Reframe Patterns
- "This isn't a failure; it's a signal."
- "This doesn't mean what you think it means."
- "You're closer than it feels."
- "The struggle is the work."
- **Tone**: perspective-shifting, compassionate
- **Use-case**: student stuck in negative interpretation

## Nervous System Language
- "Your nervous system is doing its thing."
- "This is your brain trying to protect you."
- "That fight-or-flight response makes sense here."
- "Your body is giving you information."
- **Tone**: educational, normalizing
- **Use-case**: student experiencing anxiety/stress

## Agency Restoration
- "You have more control than it feels like."
- "Let's identify what you can actually influence."
- "Here's where your leverage is."
- "You're not passive in this story."
- **Tone**: empowering, realistic
- **Use-case**: student feeling helpless

## Pattern Recognition
- "I'm noticing a pattern here."
- "This is showing up across multiple places."
- "There's a through-line in your story."
- "Let's connect these dots."
- **Tone**: observant, curious
- **Use-case**: surfacing insights
